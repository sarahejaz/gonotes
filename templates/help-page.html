Q. How does GoNotes work?

Good question! GoNotes first transcribes the video lecture. Words that are significant in the lecture, called keywords, are extracted. Then, the important parts of the lecture are extracted using textual features. After that, the amplitudes and frequencies of those important parts are observed and are used to locate other parts of the lecture that have similar amplitudes and frequencies. Thus, the key sentences from the text and the important regions from the audio are combined to make notes, which are shorter and can be consumed much faster than the lecture in its entirety.

Q. Why is there a need to analyze the audio?

That's what our research is focused on, the significance of audio in finding important parts of a lecture. The speaker uses various ways to highlight certain points that cannot be captured by the audio. That's why we're using both the audio and the transcribed text to find the accentuated parts of the lecture.

Q. Where did the idea of GoNotes come from?

We observed how long video lectures tend to be tiresome and most individuals are not able to retain lengthy videos. To cater to them, we thought to transcribe the audio of those video lectures and generate notes based on accentuated parts of the lecture. Similarly, keywords can also be of use as they highlight the main things discussed in the lecture.
